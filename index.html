Career Overview

Software developers are professionals tasked with creating computer programs and applications, encompassing the design, coding, testing, and maintenance of software systems tailored to specific requirements. They often specialize in areas such as web development, mobile app development, or software engineering. Essential skills include strong problem-solving abilities, proficiency in programming languages like Java, C++, Python, or JavaScript, meticulous attention to detail, excellent communication skills, and the capability to collaborate effectively within a team environment.

Life as a Software Dev

Responsibilities

  • Writing and debugging code
  • Collaborating with team members and stakeholders
  • Testing and maintaining software
  • Updating and improving existing software

Work Environment

  • Software developers typically work in offices, either as part of a team or independently.
  • They may also work remotely, especially in roles that require flexibility or are project-based.

Impact on Society

Software developers wield substantial influence on society, as they craft the software driving numerous facets of contemporary life. Their creations facilitate communication, entertainment, education, healthcare, and beyond, shaping the way we interact with technology. Yet, alongside these advancements come ethical and social considerations, demanding the prioritization of privacy and security within software systems to safeguard individuals and uphold societal values.

Degrees

To become a software developer, a bachelor's degree in computer science, software engineering, or a related field is typically required. Some employers may accept candidates with an associate degree or relevant certifications, but a bachelor's degree is often preferred for more advanced roles.

Earning Potential

Salary

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), the median annual wage for software developers was $130,160 in May 2023. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $58,740, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $164,520.

Outlook

The BLS projects that employment of software developers is projected to grow 25 percent from 2022 to 2032, much faster than the average for all occupations. Demand for software developers is expected to increase as organizations continue to adopt mobile devices and cloud computing.

Other Info

Software developers who work in systems software earn slightly more than those who work in applications. Industries with the highest levels of employment for software developers include computer systems design and related services, software publishers, and manufacturing.
